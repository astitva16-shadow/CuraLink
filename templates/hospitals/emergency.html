When to Call Emergency Services

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Sudden severe headache or confusion
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Signs of stroke (facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe allergic reactions

Basic First Aid Tips

For Heavy Bleeding:
  • Apply direct pressure to the wound
  • Keep the injured area elevated
  • Do not remove any embedded objects
For Burns:
  • Cool the burn with running water for 10-20 minutes
  • Do not apply ice directly
  • Cover with a clean, dry cloth
For Choking:
  • Encourage coughing
  • Perform back blows and abdominal thrusts if needed
  • Call for emergency help if unable to clear airway
For Suspected Heart Attack:
  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Have the person sit or lie down
  • Loosen any tight clothing
